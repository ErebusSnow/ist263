Those Who Keep Tradition Alive

Before you start reading, please immerse yourself in the sound of some classical music: Listen on Youtube


Classical music is not just a part of human history; it is a living art that is passed through generations. Many contemporary pianists and composers keep these traditions alive and inspire the next generation worldwide to keep discovering the timeless beauty of the piano.

Lang Lang

Lang Lang is a Chinese virtuoso who brings wonderful energy and showmanship to the piano. You can find videos of him throughout YouTube and Red Note, and almost every time you see the public happy to listen to his wonderful music. His technical brilliance combines with his emotional depth has attracted and encouraged millions of upcoming classical pianists.

Signature Repertoire

  • Tchaikovsky: Piano Concerto No. 1
  • Chopin: Études
  • Gershwin: Rhapsody in Blue
Founded the Lang Lang International Music Foundation to nurture young musicians worldwide.

Yuja Wang

Yuja Wang is renowned for her astonishing technical command and daring musical choices. She brings a fresh, contemporary energy to the concert stage. Her performances combine precision with passionate expression.

Signature Repertoire

  • Rachmaninoff: Piano Concerto No. 3
  • Prokofiev: Piano Concerto No. 2
  • Stravinsky: Three Movements from Petrushka
Multiple Grammy Award winner known for redefining concert attire and programming.

Víkingur Ólafsson

The Icelandic pianist who brings brand new interpretations of Bach and other contemporary works. His interpretations bring clarity and emotional depth, like he is not the one playing the music, but the music itself feels alive. He approaches repertoire with an intellectual curiosity and emotional transparency.

Signature Repertoire

  • Bach: Goldberg Variations
  • Philip Glass: Piano Works
  • Debussy & Rameau
His album "Goldberg Variations" won Album of the Year at the 2019 BBC Music Magazine Awards.

Daniil Trifonov

Trifonov is a Russian pianist that anyone would strive to be. With his rare combination of technical mastery and profound musical insight, critics and audiences alike praise his ability to communicate the deepest emotional content of the works he performs. He is most known for his romantic and modern repertoire interpretation, where it feels like he is speaking through these pieces.

Signature Repertoire

  • Rachmaninoff: Complete Piano Concertos
  • Chopin: Complete Études
  • Scriabin: Sonatas
Grammy Award winner (2018) and Musical America's 2019 Artist of the Year.

Alice Sara Ott

She is a German-Japanese pianist celebrated for her poetic interpretations and wide-range repertoire. Her performance showcases a balance of classical precision with spontaneous musical expression.

Signature Repertoire

  • Liszt: Transcendental Études
  • Beethoven: Piano Sonatas
  • Tchaikovsky: The Seasons
Diagnosed with multiple sclerosis in 2019, she continues to perform while raising awareness about the condition.

Seong-Jin Cho

South Korean pianist who gained international fame after winning the 2015 Chopin Competition. His playing is characterized by refined technique and natural musicality.

Signature Repertoire

  • Chopin: Complete Ballades
  • Debussy: Suite Bergamasque
  • Mozart: Piano Concertos
First South Korean to win the International Chopin Piano Competition.
